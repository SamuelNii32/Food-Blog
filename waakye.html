Delicious Waakye

Introduction to Waakye

Waakye is a traditional Ghanaian dish made with rice and beans, often accompanied by a variety of flavorful sides. The dish gets its distinctive dark color from cooking the rice and beans with dried millet leaves, which gives it both its name and unique flavor. Waakye is a beloved comfort food and is commonly enjoyed across Ghana, both as a street food and as a meal for special occasions. It's known for its simplicity yet richness in flavor, making it a perfect combination of carbs and protein.

Popular Accompaniments to Waakye

One of the defining features of waakye is the array of delicious accompaniments that are served alongside the rice and beans. It’s commonly paired with fried plantains (kelewele), boiled eggs, spaghetti, and meat such as fried fish, beef, or chicken. Additionally, waakye can also come with a side of shito, a spicy Ghanaian pepper sauce, and a range of vegetables. The variety of sides makes each serving of waakye customizable to individual preferences, offering a satisfying and balanced meal.

How to Prepare Waakye

To make waakye, start by cooking your beans (usually black-eyed peas) in water until tender. In a separate pot, prepare the rice by adding washed rice and the beans to a large pot of water. To achieve the signature dark color, add a few dried millet leaves (or dried sorghum leaves) to the pot and let everything cook together for about 30–45 minutes until the rice and beans are fully cooked. Once cooked, strain off the excess water and serve the rice and beans on a plate. Pair with fried plantains, boiled eggs, your choice of protein, and a spicy shito sauce for a truly authentic Ghanaian experience.

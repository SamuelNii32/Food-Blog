Koko & Koose

Kooko and Koose are a classic Ghanaian breakfast pairing, loved for their rich taste and nutrition. Kooko, a smooth and slightly tangy porridge made from fermented corn or millet, is served warm. Koose, crispy black-eyed pea fritters, adds a savory and spicy contrast to the porridge.

Waakye

Waakye is a Ghanaian rice and beans dish cooked with dried millet leaves, giving it a distinct color and flavor. It's commonly served with fried plantains, spaghetti, boiled eggs, and a choice of protein. Spicy shito and gari add extra texture and heat. This flavorful meal is a staple in Ghanaian cuisine.

Banku & Tilapia

Banku is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented corn and cassava dough, cooked into a smooth, stretchy paste. It's often served with grilled tilapia, accompanied by a spicy pepper sauce, and sometimes a side of fried fish. This flavorful combination is a popular meal in coastal regions of Ghana.

Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice cooked in a flavorful tomato sauce, often spiced with onions, garlic, and bell peppers. It's commonly served with grilled or fried chicken, beef, or fish. Known for its rich taste and vibrant color, Jollof rice is a staple at celebrations and gatherings in Ghana and beyond.

Ampesi

Ampesi is a traditional Ghanaian dish consisting of boiled yam, plantain, or cassava, typically served with a variety of rich and flavorful soups or stews. It’s commonly paired with groundnut soup (peanut soup), palm nut soup, or light soup.

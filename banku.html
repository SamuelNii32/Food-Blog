Delicious Banku and Tilapia

Introduction to Banku and Tilapia

Banku and tilapia is a beloved Ghanaian dish that combines the smooth, fermented corn and cassava dough of banku with the flavorful grilled or fried tilapia fish. Banku is a staple food in Ghana, known for its tangy taste and smooth texture, while tilapia is one of the most popular fish varieties in the country. The combination of the two creates a perfect balance of textures and flavors, making it a hearty and satisfying meal enjoyed across Ghana for lunch, dinner, or special occasions.

Popular Accompaniments to Banku and Tilapia

Banku and tilapia is often served with a variety of delicious accompaniments to enhance its flavor. The dish is typically paired with spicy shito sauce, a hot pepper sauce that adds depth and heat. It can also be served with a side of tomato-based stew, garden egg stew, or a variety of vegetables. Some people enjoy it with a fresh side of salad or fried plantains to round out the meal, offering a combination of savory, spicy, and fresh elements that complement the main dish perfectly.

How to Prepare Banku and Tilapia

To prepare banku, begin by mixing cornmeal and cassava flour with water in a pot over medium heat. Stir constantly until the mixture thickens and becomes smooth. Once it reaches a smooth, dough-like consistency, reduce the heat and continue stirring until it's fully cooked. For the tilapia, season the fish with a mixture of salt, pepper, and spices, then grill or fry it until golden brown and crispy. Serve the banku on a plate alongside the tilapia and pair with shito sauce or your choice of stew. This combination is a satisfying, flavorful, and authentic Ghanaian meal.
